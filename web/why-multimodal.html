PrismRAG

Why a visual retriever beats text-only RAG on figures.

Chat Inspection Papers Figures Why multimodal?

The answer is in the picture. Text-only search never sees it.

When the fact you want lives inside a chart or a screenshot, searching the text around it isn't enough. The page that holds the answer never makes the shortlist. PrismRAG adds a visual retriever that ranks the page images themselves, so the right page comes back.

Ask "In Figure 111, what's the price of the keyboard?" and text-only retrieval can't find the page. The visual retriever pulls the exact page where $49.99 sits in the screenshot.

What you're looking at. These cases replay PrismRAG's committed MMLongBench retrieval results. For each question, the text-only retriever missed the gold page (it isn't in its top 10) and the visual router found it. The hosted demo runs on CPU, so the visual leg (ColQwen2) is turned off there and only text-only retrieval is live; these results come from a run on a local GPU. Numbers come straight from data/eval/baseline-mmlongbench-text.json and baseline-mmlongbench-router.json.
